01 Introduction

Raising capital is harder than it should be.

A founder with a strong business often spends six to nine months running an outreach motion that pre-supposes warm intros, generic decks, and the hope that the right investor will surface. Most do not. The ones who do tend to be a function of network, not narrative.

Rogue Divisions is a specialist capital advisory firm headquartered in London with global coverage across Europe, the Americas, APAC, and the GCC. We help founders raise equity, primarily from Seed through Series B, with select work at growth and lower-middle-market. We are now sector agnostic, with deeper specialism in deep-tech, fintech, climate and energy, life sciences, consumer, and food and agtech.

The numbers that anchor the firm are simple. We have run more than eighty mandates across twenty-eight countries. Our investor network spans seventeen thousand active VC, PE, family office, HNW, and sovereign relationships, with aggregate dry powder above $1.1 trillion. Clients we have worked with collectively represent more than $100 billion in enterprise value.

The point of those numbers is not the size. The point is the precision of the access. The same network applied to the wrong investor segment produces noise. The work we do is making sure your raise is in front of the people whose mandate, stage, geography, and thesis already align with the deal.

The firm grew out of marketing operations that had run high-performance campaigns for global brands and venture-backed startups before turning the same instinct on the private capital markets. The capital markets are largely stuck on a previous generation of operating practice: static investor lists, generic blast emails, advisor relationships that have not been re-evaluated in a decade. We treat raises like product launches. Segment the audience. Build the asset. Run the campaign. Iterate against live signal. Close.

That instinct, applied with the discipline of capital markets compliance and the operational scale of an AI-forward stack, is the firm.

02 The state of capital raising

Two patterns repeat in conversations with founders who come to us after running their own process.

First, the volume problem.

Founders are sending the same pitch to investor lists that were not built around their deal. The reply rate is low because the targeting is generic. The signal is buried under noise. By the time a founder has burned through a hundred warm intros and a thousand cold emails, the remaining capacity is exhausted and morale is lower than the cap table can sustain.

Second, the narrative problem.

The deck reads like the founder is in the room presenting it, but the founder is not in the room. Investors get five minutes with the asset. If the document does not communicate the thesis, the unit economics, the moat, and the path to the next round in the first thirty seconds, the deal is filed. Most decks are written for the founder, not the investor. Most teasers are too long, too generic, or too vague to drive a meeting.

These two problems compound. A poorly targeted list with a poorly framed asset produces poor outcomes regardless of how strong the underlying business is. The fix is not more activity. The fix is alignment between message and audience.

That alignment is what we call Investor-Message Fit.

03 Our approach

Investor-Message Fit.

Investors filter capital deployment against two questions:

  1. Why should I believe in this business?
  2. How do I reduce my risk in deploying into it?

The answer to the first question is narrative grounded in evidence. The answer to the second is matching the deal to investors whose existing thesis, sector, and stage already make this deal underwritable. Most fundraising effort fails because it answers one of these questions, not both.

Investor-Message Fit is the discipline of getting both right at the same time. It sits at the intersection of five components, and we work across all of them in parallel.

01 · Narrative

Your thesis distilled into materials an investor can underwrite in fifty minutes.

A sharper deck, a teaser that drives meetings rather than describes the business, a data room organised the way diligence actually flows, and a teaser-deck-meeting funnel where each step earns the next. On nuanced deals, narrative gives an IC something they can take to their LPs and defend, even when the deal does not match their previous twenty deployments. That is craft.

02 · Investor segment

Not a list. A mandate-aware target set.

Built from stage, sector, geography, cheque size, deal type, portfolio fit, and thesis alignment. Database integrations, network mapping, and live deal-flow signals identify investors actively deploying into deals like yours, not investors who deployed into something adjacent in 2019. Outreach is then prioritised in waves; the strongest-fit investors are reserved for the moment the materials and process are tight.

03 · Channel

Coordinated outreach across the channels investors actually pay attention to.

Direct email, LinkedIn, warm introductions, and where appropriate, paid distribution. The infrastructure handles deliverability, sender reputation, sequencing, and personalisation at scale, so the message arrives in the inbox rather than the spam folder, and lands as a tailored note rather than an obvious template. We do not blast. A campaign that books fifteen of the right investors beats one that books a hundred wrong ones.

04 · Presentation

The materials and the founder both ready for the meeting the campaign generates.

We refine the pitch deck and supporting collateral against real investor feedback, coach delivery, build out objection-handling for the questions we are seeing on calls, and prepare the data room so that diligence is fast rather than fraught. We sit on calls when useful. We do not sit on calls when not.

05 · Optimisation loop

Every investor meeting produces signal. We capture it.

Which questions came up, which objections recurred, which segments are converting at a higher rate, where the narrative is strong, and where it needs another pass. We feed that signal back into the materials, the targeting, and the messaging, and run the next wave better than the last. A first-wave reply rate is data. A second-wave reply rate that is double the first is leverage.

Together, these five components are the operating system of a Rogue Divisions raise.

04 Why it works

Separate the two halves of the investor decision.

On belief

Belief is built by evidence framed inside a narrative the investor can repeat. We work with you to surface the strongest data your business has, including unit economics, retention, market validation, and proof of scale where applicable, and structure it inside a story that maps to what investors at your stage are pattern-matching against. Where the data is incomplete, we run targeted campaigns to gather it. Where the story is unclear, we restructure it. The result is materials that pass the first scan and survive the second meeting.

On risk

Risk is reduced by counterparty fit, not by claims of low risk. An investor underwriting a deal in their existing thesis carries less perceived risk than the same investor underwriting a deal that requires them to bend their mandate. Our targeting work makes sure your deal is in front of the investors for whom this is a familiar shape, not an exception. Where the deal does require some calibration on the investor side, the materials provide the rationale they need to defend the decision internally.

On structure

There is a second-order effect. Finding the right lead, not just any cheque, sets the rest of the round up to close. The right lead validates the deal for follow-on capital. The right cap table is structurally built to support the next round before you need to raise it. We work with founders on cap table construction, not just on capital identification.

Combined, those three layers shorten the time to a strong term sheet and improve the terms of the round when it arrives.

07 Our process

How we get there in practice.

The five-component framework above is what we are working towards. Buildout typically takes four to six weeks from kickoff to first wave; outreach and refinement run in parallel from that point through close.

01

Discovery and strategy

  • Deep working session on the business model, market position, competitive landscape, growth thesis, and existing capital position.
  • Identification of investor objections we expect to see and the reinforcement work needed to pre-empt them.
  • Capital-raising strategy: which investors, which channels, which materials, which sequence, which milestones.
02

Materials and data

  • Audit of existing pitch deck, data room, financial model, and any supporting collateral.
  • Refinement or rebuild of pitch materials, teaser document, executive summary, and data room structure.
  • Where applicable, targeted data acquisition campaigns to gather the metrics investors will ask for: customer acquisition cost, lifetime value, retention curves, market validation.
  • A unified teaser document that anchors all outbound messaging.
03

Investor research

  • Mandate-driven targeting across our investor network and database integrations: stage, sector, geography, cheque size, deal type, portfolio fit, recent deployment activity.
  • A curated, prioritised investor list segmented into tiers, with the strongest-fit investors reserved for the moment process and materials are most calibrated.
04

Outreach and appointment setting

  • Multi-wave outreach across email, LinkedIn, warm introductions, and where appropriate, paid distribution.
  • Personalisation at scale through our outreach infrastructure: each investor receives a message calibrated to their mandate, recent investments, and known thesis.
  • Meetings booked directly to the founder's calendar.
  • Discreet handling of compliance and regulatory considerations through our broker-dealer chaperone partnerships where the deal type requires it.
05

Iteration and close support

  • Coaching on pitch delivery and objection-handling between meetings.
  • Recording and analysis of investor calls to identify recurring themes.
  • Continuous refinement of materials, targeting, and messaging based on signal from each wave.
  • Support through term sheet, diligence, and close. We are partners through the round, not transactional through the first meeting.

09 Is Rogue Divisions right for you?

A strong fit in some situations. A wrong fit in others.

Right partner, if

  • You are raising equity at Seed, Series A, or Series B with an established business or a credible thesis backed by data, and you have a clear use of proceeds.
  • You have run a process and the targeting feels off. You are getting meetings with investors who do not understand the deal, or you are not getting meetings at all because the list was not built around your specifics.
  • Your deal needs positioning work. The business is strong but does not sit cleanly inside one investor bucket, and you need someone who can translate it into language each segment of investors can underwrite.
  • You value process and infrastructure. You want to know what is happening, when, and what the data is showing, rather than waiting for a monthly update from an advisor on a static retainer.
  • You are looking for a partner, not a list. You want someone who will work the deal end to end and stays with you through the close, not someone who hands over an investor spreadsheet and waits for an introduction request.

Wrong partner, if

  • You are pre-product and pre-thesis with no data and no clear use of proceeds. We are not the right firm to help you find the business.
  • You are looking exclusively for cheap angel cheques inside your existing network. The marginal value of our process is lower in that situation.
  • You are not prepared to commit time to building the materials and the targeting strategy with us in the first four to six weeks. The work compounds; the buildout matters.
10 Commercial structure

Three legs of a stool.

Our pricing is built on three components. We treat them as three legs of a stool: increasing one allows the others to come down. We are flexible on the mix, deliberately, because every deal is structured differently and the alignment that works for a $5M Seed is not the alignment that works for a $50M Series B.

01 · Setup fee

Upfront, paid at start of engagement.

$10k – $25k

Covers compliance and regulatory work, due diligence on our side, materials development, investor list curation, outreach infrastructure, analyst time, and the buildout phase that gets the campaign ready to go live. Not a retainer. Hard cost we incur on day one and recover on day one.

02 · Success fee

A percentage of capital raised, paid on close.

2 – 4% equity
1 – 2% debt

Paid on capital that comes through our process. We do not take exclusive mandates. Where appropriate, success fees can be split with co-advisors in our network when capital is raised through their relationships.

03 · Warrants

A portion of compensation as warrants.

0.5 – 1.5%

Where useful, structured at the most recent round price, prorated to capital raised or time-vested over twelve to twenty-four months. Aligns our incentives with the long-term success of the business and reduces cash burden.

How the three legs interact

A founder who wants to minimise upfront cash can shift weight onto success fee or warrants. A founder who wants to minimise dilution and equity-linked compensation can shift weight onto upfront and success. A founder who wants to maximise cash alignment can keep all three at lower levels and pay nothing extra unless we deliver. Every engagement is shaped to the deal.

Engagements are typically structured as six-month initial terms with renewal terms agreed if the round runs longer. We frame engagements with a view to multiple rounds, because the strongest outcomes are when we are partners with you across this round and the next, not transactional through one transaction.
